The Methodology

We do not just make these numbers up. We take this joke very seriously. Our baselines are aggregated from the following authoritative demographic datasets:

  • Parents & Grandparents: Base models derived from the Population Reference Bureau's estimate of 117 billion modern humans born since 190,000 B.C.E., adjusted for historical 50% childhood mortality and reproductive variance.
  • Extended Lineage: Multipliers calculated using historical sibling survival matrixes and kinship models from the Max Planck Institute for Demographic Research.
  • Blended Families: Factoring in 18th and 19th-century widowhood/remarriage rates, cross-referenced with modern Pew Research blended family tracking.
  • Pet Parents: Domestication milestones cross-referenced with companion animal statistics from UK Pet Food and historical feline/canine population estimates.
Rank = B₁₉₀₀ + ∑ [ y = 1900 → target_year ] ( G_y × λ ) + F

B₁₉₀₀ = Pre-calculated historical base up to 1900
G_y = Global demographic birth volume for year y
λ = Role-specific minting probability
F = Fractional completion of the target year

*Note: Post-1900 demographic curve integration utilizes interpolated data points anchored by UN decadal census estimates.
